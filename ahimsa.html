ahimsa


Ahimsa, (Sanskrit: “noninjury”) in the Indian religions of Jainism, Hinduism, and Buddhism, the ethical principle of not causing harm to other living things.

During the nonviolence movement by M.K.Gandhi he started a fight against the british in which he rallied with many freedom fighters and common people through protesting.The british irritated by this act brutally tortured gandhi and his supporters.But gandhi did not kill or harm the british.He went against the british but without violence.This is the true meaning of ahimsa.It means that we should not harm any living beings.Gandhi inspired many people to practice ahimsa.Beause of him and his beliefs our country is on its way to become a violence free country.
